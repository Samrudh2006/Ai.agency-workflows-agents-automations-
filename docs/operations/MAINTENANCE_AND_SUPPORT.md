# 🛠️ Maintenance & Support SOP
**How to fulfill the Monthly Retainer and provide ongoing value.**

</div>

<br />

The agency makes its highest profit margin on the monthly retainer. To prevent churn, clients must feel they are getting proactive value, rather than just "paying for server hosting."

---

## 📅 The Monthly "Proactive" Deliverable

Every month, the Account Manager is responsible for performing a **Chat Log Audit** and sending a Loom video to the client.

### 1. The Audit Process (Day 15 of the month)
- Log into the client's Voice Agent or Chatbot dashboard (e.g., Vapi, Supabase).
- Export the last 200 conversations.
- Look for **"Failure Points"**:
  - Did the AI apologize frequently for not knowing something?
  - Was there a question asked 10+ times that isn't answered on the client's website?

### 2. The Fix
- Update the AI's Knowledge Base (upload a new PDF or update the System Prompt) to handle the new questions.

### 3. The Loom Video Script
*Send this video to the client to prove ROI:*

> "Hey [Client], doing our monthly audit of the AI Agent. Good news: it handled 145 conversations this month, saving your team about 25 hours. 
> 
> I noticed 12 people asked about your holiday hours, which the AI didn't know. I proactively updated the system prompt to include that schedule, so going forward, those questions will be handled instantly. Let me know if you need anything else added!"

## 🐛 Bug Fixes & Service Level Agreement (SLA)

If an automation breaks (e.g., Make.com throws an error), our SLA kicks in:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** The webhook is down, leads are being lost. 
  - *Response Time:* < 2 hours.
- **Severity 2 (Moderate):** A specific function mapping is wrong (e.g., Last Name is populating into the First Name field).
  - *Response Time:* < 24 hours.
- **Severity 3 (Low):** The client wants to change the wording of a specific prompt instruction.
  - *Response Time:* < 48 hours.

All clients must submit tickets through our dedicated support portal or the shared Slack channel.
