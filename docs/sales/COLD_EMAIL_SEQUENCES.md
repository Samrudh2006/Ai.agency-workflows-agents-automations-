# 📧 Cold Email Sequences
**High-converting outbound templates to get Discovery Calls.**

</div>

<br />

The goal of cold email is NOT to sell your AI agency. The goal is to sell the meeting. Keep it under 75 words. No fluff.

---

## ✉️ Sequence 1: The "Missed Calls" Angle (For Local Biz/Real Estate)

**Subject:** Missed calls after hours?

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I was looking at your website and noticed you don't have a 24/7 answering system. A lot of agencies in [City] are losing high-ticket clients because leads call at 8 PM, get voicemail, and call the next competitor.

We build AI Voice Receptionists that sound completely human, answer immediately 24/7, and book appointments right into your calendar.

Would you be open to a 10-minute demo call where I let you speak live with one of our AI agents? 

Best,
[Your Name]

---

## ✉️ Sequence 2: The "CRM Chaos" Angle (For B2B/SaaS/Agencies)

**Subject:** Quick question about your lead flow

**Body:**
Hey [First Name],

When a new lead fills out a form on the [Company] site, how much manual data entry is your sales team doing right now?

We help marketing agencies automate their entire backend (Lead capture → CRM update → Personalized AI Email follow-up) so your team stops doing manual copy-pasting.

Our clients typically save 15-20 hours a week of admin work.

Open to seeing a quick 1-pager on how this works? 

Best,
[Your Name]

---

## ✉️ Sequence 3: The "Customer Support Deflection" Angle (E-commerce)

**Subject:** "Where is my order?" tickets

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I'm assuming your support inbox gets slammed with repetitive questions like "where is my order" or "what is your return policy?"

We build custom AI Support Agents specifically trained on your Shopify/WooCommerce data. They instantly answer 60-70% of routine tickets, looking up order statuses autonomously.

It acts like an extra full-time support rep, but works 24/7 for a fraction of the cost.

Do you have 5 minutes to see a prototype I built for [Company]?

Best,
[Your Name]

---

## 💡 Best Practices for Cold Emailing
1. **Never send links in the first email.** It hurts deliverability. 
2. **Follow-up is crucial.** Send the second email 3 days later: *"Just bumping this up. Let me know if you're open to seeing the prototype!"*
3. **Personalization works.** If you can reference a specific thing on their website, do it.
